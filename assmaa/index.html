About Me

I am an international computer science student with a primary interest in cybersecurity and system-level software. My work focuses on understanding how vulnerabilities arise in real systems and how they can be analyzed, exploited, and mitigated through careful design and experimentation.

I am more interested in backend systems, security mechanisms, and low-level behavior than front-end development. Programming, for me, is a way to analyze system behavior, evaluate security assumptions, and improve the robustness of software systems.

Background & Interests

My project experience is mainly in cybersecurity and systems-related work. I have completed hands-on security labs and academic projects involving network attacks, operating system mechanisms, and vulnerability analysis. These experiences have strengthened my understanding of how real systems behave under adversarial conditions.

Beyond technical work, I have a strong interest in continuous learning and personal development. I speak multiple languages and enjoy using them in academic and cross-cultural environments. In my free time, I play the guitar, stay active through sports and fitness activities, and enjoy learning new skills both inside and outside of computer science.

Career Skill Development Plan

My current goal is to further develop practical cybersecurity skills by working on more advanced attack and defense scenarios, particularly in operating systems, networking, and secure software design. I aim to deepen my understanding of how security theory translates into real-world implementations.

In the long term, I plan to pursue advanced study or research in cybersecurity, with a focus on vulnerability analysis, system security, and secure architecture design. My objective is to build expertise that allows me to assess and improve the security of complex software systems.
